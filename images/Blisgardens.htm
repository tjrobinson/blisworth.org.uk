04-00   An early picture of a large lawn area at Blisworth Gardens, with the Lido swimming area in the background (see below)

04-01  This picture shows the swimming pool area of Blisworth gardens.  Called the "Lido", it was established by the third owner of the gardens in about 1930.  As shown, it is how many villagers remember it.  By the mid-1930's the school regularly sent children here for swimming lessons.  1947 aerial photos (NRO) show that this pool was near to the Station Road and at a lowest point - see 04-13 below.

04-02 The gardens were however set up by the first owner, Thomas Shaw shortly after opening his new hotel in 1845.  The second owner Charles Parker re-opened the gardens in 1879 with a lavish fete and gala as described in this poster of the times.  The gardens were an attraction, especially for one day at the weekends, for people even from neighbouring counties.  There was a Victorian realisation that clean air and exercise, away from the dirty busy towns, was beneficial.  More information  In the early part of the 20th century there were some acts of kindness towards the poor.  In 1912 there were poor put up in the old Towcester workhouse and here is a letter sent out to the manager.

04-12 The gardens fell into disuse by 1950 once railway holidays were giving way to the motorcar.  Around 1958 the 4 acre area was largely cleared and made available for caravans for workers on the M1 motorway.  By 1960 there were 70 caravan type dwellings.  Later it developed into a park for modest-sized homes.
