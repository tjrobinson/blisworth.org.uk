Thomas Shaw, proprietor of the Angel Hotel, Bridge Street, Northampton was quick
to expand his coaching business and link it to the railways - this poster and the next
are dated 1839.  A mention of Blisworth Station implies the old station at the arch.

An extract from a traveller's report, from Thomas Roscoe's 1839 description of the
main line going north, "...upon emerging the Blisworth Cutting (sic - Roade Cutting) ...
will not fail to observe the square tower of Blisworth Church peering to the west ...
we pass over a lofty bridge of one arch, which spans the high road between the towns
of Towcester and Northampton, and at this point there is a covered staircase leading
from the railway to Blisworth Station, which is of second class (meaning that not all
trains will stop there).
